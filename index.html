❓ NavPlotter Help

Getting Started

1. Load a chart — Tap Maps to open the library. Choose a built-in WAC chart or import your own JPG from your device.

2. Calibrate scale — Tap Scale and drag the two handles to span exactly 10 NM on the chart. Choose SEC (1:500K) or WAC (1:1M).

3. Calibrate north — Tap North and align the two handles along a meridian (longitude line). This tells the plotter where true north is.

Navigation Plotter

Show/Hide — Tap Plotter in the toolbar.

Move — Drag the ruler body to reposition.

Rotate body — Drag the blue handle at the right end, or use two fingers on the ruler.

Rotate compass — Spin the compass ring with one finger.

Align compass north — Double-tap the compass to snap it to chart north.

Lock — Tap Lock to freeze the plotter in place.

Read heading — The bottom panel shows True Course and Magnetic Heading (if variation is set).

Route Planning

Add waypoints — Tap the pencil tool, then tap the chart. Drag to fine-tune position (magnifier appears). A bottom sheet lets you choose the point type and name.

Point types: ▲ Departure (green) | ● Waypoint (cyan) | ◆ Checkpoint (yellow) | ■ Destination (red)

Auto-connect — New points are automatically connected to the previous point.

Snap-to-line — When placing a point near an existing leg, it snaps to the line and splits it (orange dashed guide appears).

Draw line manually — Tap the ruler tool, then tap two points to connect them.

Select — Tap a point or leg to select it (shown in orange).

Long-press — Hold a point or leg for 0.4s to open the context menu with Edit, Move, Insert Point, or Delete.

Insert waypoint on leg — Long-press a leg and choose "Insert Point" to split it in half.

Leg Parameters

Select a leg to see its parameter panel: Altitude, IAS, TAS, OAT, Magnetic Variation, Wind direction/speed, Fuel flow.

Auto TAS — When IAS and altitude are set, TAS auto-computes (+2%/1000ft). If OAT is also set, density altitude formula is used. TAS turns green when auto-computed.

Apply All — Copies the current leg's parameters to all legs.

Route Management

Save/Load — Routes are saved per chart in IndexedDB. Tap Save to persist, Load to open the route list.

Reverse — 🔄 swaps departure and destination, reversing the route order.

Duplicate — Creates a copy of the current route.

Undo/Redo — Up to 50 steps. Also Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y on keyboard.

VFR Nav Log

Tap Nav Log to see a full navigation log table: Leg, Alt, IAS, TAS, TC, Var, WCA, MH, Dist, GS, ETE, Fuel, cumulative columns.

Route fields — Frequencies, Notes, Fuel Reserve (30 or 45 min), ETD (adds ETA column).

Drag to reorder legs — Long-press a row (or grab the ☰ handle) and drag to a new position.

Print — Open Print options to generate a printable page with the annotated chart and nav log table, or export as text file.

Distance Overlay

Tap the eye (👁) toggle in the tools panel. Shows compact leg labels on all legs plus cumulative/remaining NM badges at each waypoint. "DEST" appears in green at the destination.

In-Flight Mode

Tap Fly to start a flight timer. A blue bar appears showing:

  • Elapsed time — running clock since departure
  • Active leg — current leg name and number
  • Drift — ahead/behind planned ETE (green = early, orange = late)
  • Mark — tap the checkmark button when you pass each checkpoint to advance to the next leg

The active leg is highlighted in blue on the chart. Tap Stop to end the flight.

Actual times — Each time you tap Mark, the actual leg duration is recorded. These appear on canvas labels, tooltips, the nav log (blue "Actual" column), print output, and text export.

Clear flight data — Open Nav Log and tap the red Clear Flight Data button to erase all recorded times. Starting a new flight also prompts to clear previous data.

Start from a specific point — Select a point before tapping Fly to start the flight from that point instead of the departure.

Chart Display

Pan — Drag the chart, or hold Space + drag (keyboard), or middle-click drag.

Zoom — Pinch, or scroll wheel.

Brightness — Tap Bright to show a slider (20%–200%) for cockpit readability.

Tips

Swipe down on the point creation sheet to dismiss it.

Hover over a leg (desktop) or tap a leg to see full details in a tooltip.

The route summary bar at the top shows total distance, ETE, and fuel at a glance.

NavPlotter works offline once loaded — it's a PWA you can install on your home screen.
